Overview

Design as way of thinking

For more than two years I collaborated with Marca Motors Mitsubishi, a dealership with strong influence in Northern Brazil, particularly in regions closely connected to the agrobusiness sector.

My work covered a broad spectrum of design activities — from high-volume marketing campaigns and social media content to print materials and internal communication. Beyond producing visuals, I also helped introduce design thinking into operational processes, connecting design with departments like CRM, sales, IT, and accounting.

Unlike many of my other projects where I led the entire creative direction, this experience required me to work inside a fast-paced marketing team, adapting to multiple stakeholders, short deadlines, and hierarchical decision-making.

This project became less about individual visual authorship and more about design as a collaborative business tool.

Context

A different breed

Marca Motors operates in a region where agrobusiness plays a central economic role. While the manufacturer’s national marketing strategies divide their focus between urban and rural audiences, the dealership developed a strong positioning by deepening its relationship with agricultural entrepreneurs and workers.

Problems

Challenges we needed to solve.

The rebrand needed to address several core issues that were limiting the firm's growth and perception in the market.

Outdated Visual Identity

The existing logo and materials looked generic and did not differentiate the brand from competitors in the accounting space.

Lack of Consistency

Brand assets were applied inconsistently across channels, weakening recognition and trust with clients.

Low Digital Presence

The brand had no cohesive digital identity, making it hard to establish credibility on social media and web platforms.

Approachability Gap

The firm wanted to attract younger entrepreneurs but the brand felt overly corporate and unapproachable.

Challenge

Create a brand system that feels professional yet approachable — bridging trust with personality.

Design Approach

Guiding principles.

Clarity First

Every design decision prioritized clear communication — from typography choices to color contrast — ensuring the brand speaks with confidence.

Flexible System

The identity was built as a modular system that adapts seamlessly from business cards to social media, websites, and presentations.

Human Touch

Warm colors, rounded forms, and friendly typography bring personality to a traditionally rigid industry, making the brand feel welcoming.

Result

The final identity.

Takeaways

What I learned.

Client Collaboration

Close collaboration with the client at every stage ensured the final product truly represented their vision and values.

System Thinking

Designing a scalable brand system rather than isolated assets made the identity more resilient and easier to maintain long-term.

Industry Context

Understanding the accounting industry's expectations helped strike the right balance between innovation and professionalism.
